# Source-material reuse permissions

The diploma's source materials in `original_materials/` came out of a multi-TAFE collaboration in which Kangan (Tim's employer) was a participant, and the working assumption is that Kangan's participation grants reuse rights over all of that content — including units authored by other TAFEs. That assumption is still being confirmed with Kangan's legal team, so this document records the assumption, why we hold it, how to apply it day-to-day, and the fallback plan if legal denies reuse. Paths are relative to the `diploma-cloud-cyber-content/` repo root.

## Origin: a multi-TAFE collaboration

The materials in `original_materials/` are the output of a multi-TAFE collaboration in which Kangan was a participant. Each participating TAFE authored a different subset of the 21 units in the Diploma of IT (Cloud and Cyber Security). Examples in the source bear this out: the YAT College case study is attributed to Melbourne Polytechnic (Richard Neumann, 2021); other units carry other TAFE attributions.

## Working assumption

**Working assumption (per Tim):** Kangan has permission to reuse all materials in `original_materials/` as a collaboration participant, including content authored by other TAFEs.

This is a working assumption, not a confirmed legal position — Tim is double-checking it with the Kangan legal team. [TBD — needs confirmation: Kangan legal sign-off on reuse rights over other TAFEs' content.]

## Why

Tim confirmed that the multi-TAFE collaboration origin explains the disjointed authoring style we are now refactoring: different TAFEs wrote different units with no shared spine. He also confirmed that Kangan's participation gives them reuse rights. He is double-checking the assumption with the Kangan legal team.

## How to apply

- Treat all `original_materials/` content as reusable across cluster authoring — including verbatim quotation, paraphrasing, rebranding, and scenario reframing.
- Don't worry about attribution-blocking; content can be ported across as needed.
- Flag any action that would be hard to reverse if the legal assumption turns out to be wrong (e.g. publishing externally, sharing with non-Kangan parties) and check with Tim first.
- This working assumption is also stated in [project-overview.md](project-overview.md) § Background as the project-level statement.

## Fallback if legal denies reuse

If legal denies reuse, reauthor affected materials under a different name with equivalent but distinct content. Tim's standing instruction is "reuse existing material wherever possible" — the fallback only kicks in if a specific item is blocked.

Preserve structural shape (scenario type, document set, AT mappings) so the rest of the cluster work is unaffected. The 3-AT architectural decisions, the scenario-spine approach, the contextual reflective-question framing, and the document inventory do not depend on any specific source identity and stay stable across this contingency. This fallback is also stated in [project-overview.md](project-overview.md) § Background.
