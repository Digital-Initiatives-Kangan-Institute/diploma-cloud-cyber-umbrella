When guiding Tim through a **hands-on, interactive procedure** (AWS Academy / console navigation, deploys, any GUI click-path), give **one step at a time**, then wait for him to report what he sees before giving the next.

**Why:** something almost always differs from what I predicted (a button is named differently, an error appears, the screen isn't what I assumed) within the first step or two — so steps 3–10 of a long list are wasted, and he has to come back anyway. His words: "you always give me 10 steps and I always end up only doing 1 or 2 before needing to come back… steps 3-10 are a waste because there is always a problem before we get to them."

**How to apply:** for interactive walk-throughs, reply with the single next action + "tell me what you see". Resist the urge to pre-write the whole sequence even when I'm confident — the feedback loop is faster and avoids wasted output. (This is about live click-by-click guidance; multi-step *plans* I execute myself with tools are fine to lay out in full.)
